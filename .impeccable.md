## Design Context

### Users
Agora Dashboard serves human operators who govern multi-agent task execution. They use it to observe task state, inspect runtime behavior, arbitrate decisions, and keep delivery auditable rather than chat-driven.

### Brand Personality
Precise, authoritative, and quietly experimental. The interface should feel trustworthy and high-control first, but with enough design conviction that it does not collapse into generic SaaS admin or default AI aesthetics.

### Aesthetic Direction
The Dashboard now adopts a single canonical visual direction: `C-Sharp`.

`C-Sharp` keeps the product in a blue-white family, but explicitly rejects the overused AI cyan control-panel look, purple accents, soft glassmorphism, and generic rounded SaaS cards.

`C-Sharp` means:

- mineral blue-white instead of default bright cyan
- rectilinear shell, hard cuts, narrow seams, and crisp panel segmentation
- paper, fog, slate, and sage-tinted neutrals instead of sterile grayscale
- warmth used only as a precise judgment or escalation signal
- an overall feeling closer to a modern governance console or editorial systems desk than a generic admin template

The experience should feel sharp, calm, and exacting, not playful or soft.

### Design Principles
1. Keep governance legible: hierarchy and status signaling matter more than ornamental effects.
2. Replace default AI cyan with mineral blue-white, sage-tinted neutrals, and rarer accent logic.
3. Preserve the hard-edged rectilinear system language; no drift back toward rounded dashboard conventions.
4. Use contrast through structure, typography, seams, and material finish, not through louder saturation or decorative glow.
5. Preserve operator trust: even when the visual language becomes fresher, it must still read as authoritative and usable.
6. Favor distinctive restraint over trendy spectacle.
